Cloning



What is Cloning?
Cloning is taking genetic copies of single dna cells. Clones can either occur naturally in identical twins or it can be done in a laboratory.

How is it done?
DNA cloning is done in a lab, by taking cells from animals and placing them into an unfertilized egg that has had its dna removed from it. The egg then develops naturally into an embryo, and develops the same way that a normal egg would develop.

How was cloning invented?
Genetic cloning has been around for a long period of time, in the form of cloning plants, however cloning of animals was not possible until the 1970’s with Dolly the sheep. Cloning of plants had been around for hundreds or even thousands of years.

What animals are cloned?
•Dogs
•Cats
•Successful sports horses bulls that are used for fighting
•Endangered or extinct species
•Farm animals such as cows, and bulls- these farm animals are then used for food
